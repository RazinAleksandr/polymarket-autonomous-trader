## Rules (Apply Before Every Crypto Bet)

### Rule 1 -- Price Target Markets: Settlement Mechanics First
**Rule**: Like commodities, crypto price target markets (e.g., "BTC above $100k by Dec 31") resolve against specific price feeds. Always check:
- Which exchange price is used (Coinbase, Binance, Kraken, CMC composite?)
- Is it spot or futures?
- Is it close price, high, or VWAP?
**Apply when**: Any crypto price target market.

### Rule 2 -- Macro Correlation During Risk-Off Events
**Rule**: Crypto is highly correlated with risk-off macro events (war, financial crisis, Fed tightening). During geopolitical crises, BTC may be suppressed by risk-off even as oil surges.
**Implication**: Don't bet bullish crypto during geopolitical crises unless specifically crypto-positive news (ETF approval, halving, regulatory win).

### Rule 3 -- Regulatory Markets Have Long Timelines
**Rule**: Markets for "SEC approves X" or "Congress passes crypto law" almost always take longer than the market implies. US regulatory timelines are unpredictable and subject to political gridlock.
**Implication**: On regulatory approval markets with deadlines, lean NO unless the regulatory process is clearly well advanced (e.g., final rule published).

### Rule 4 -- Halving Cycle Effects
**Rule**: BTC halvings (historically every ~4 years) have preceded significant price increases 6-18 months later. Factor in where we are in the halving cycle when estimating price probabilities.
**Caveat**: Past cycles don't guarantee future performance, but base rates are useful for calibration.

### Rule 5 -- Don't Chase After Large Moves
**Rule**: After a 30%+ move in BTC/ETH, markets for "will it continue above X" are often overpriced because of recency bias among market makers. Mean reversion is more common than continuation.
**Apply when**: Crypto price target markets opened during or immediately after a large spike.

## Markets to Research

When scanning crypto, prioritize:
- BTC/ETH price targets with > $10k volume
- Regulatory events with clear binary outcomes
- Exchange-specific events (listings, hacks, regulatory actions)
- Stablecoin depegging events (usually good NO opportunities)
