## Rules (Apply Before Every Finance Bet)

### Rule 1 -- NEAR-CERTAINTY FINANCIAL EVENTS HAVE NO EDGE
**Rule**: When Fed/ECB decisions have 99%+ consensus (CME FedWatch), there is no edge to capture. The 0.3pp upside is negative EV after costs.
**Apply when**: Any central bank rate decision market where consensus exceeds 95%.
**Taught by**: Fed rate March 2026 at 99.7% -- correctly passed, zero edge available.

### Rule 2 -- SETTLEMENT MECHANICS APPLY TO INDICES TOO
**Rule**: SPX close, GDP official release, CPI print -- all have specific timing and measurement methodology. Verify which data source the market uses for resolution.
**Apply when**: Any financial index or economic data target market.
**Key details**:
- S&P 500: official closing price vs intraday high can differ significantly on volatile days
- GDP: advance estimate vs final revision can differ by 0.5-1.0pp
- CPI: seasonally adjusted vs unadjusted, month-over-month vs year-over-year
- Employment: NFP initial release vs revised figures

### Rule 3 -- Earnings Season Creates Correlated Volatility
**Rule**: During earnings season (Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct), index-level markets are subject to correlated moves from mega-cap reports. A single AAPL/NVDA miss can move SPX by 2-3%.
**Apply when**: Index target markets during earnings season weeks.

## Lessons Learned

### Near-Certainty Markets (March 2026)
Fed rate decision at 99.7% consensus demonstrated that near-certainty events in financial markets have zero tradeable edge. The 0.3pp potential upside is consumed by transaction costs and the tiny but nonzero probability of a surprise. This lesson generalizes: any financial market priced above 97% should be passed.

### Settlement vs Intraday Applies to Finance
The commodity lesson (CME settlement != intraday high) extends to financial indices. SPX official close, GDP official release, and CPI print all have specific measurement methodologies that may differ from the headline number or intraday extremes.
