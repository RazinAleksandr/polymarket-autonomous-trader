## Rules (Apply Before Every Entertainment Bet)

### Rule 1 -- CHECK ALL OPTIONS (Critical)
**Date learned**: 2026-03-15
**Rule**: Before betting on any entertainment category, list ALL nominees/options and their market prices. Never anchor on just the top 2 frontrunners.
**Why**: Bugonia (Yorgos Lanthimos) was priced at ~2-5% and won Best Picture 2026. We bet on Sinners (24% estimate) and didn't even notice Bugonia.
**Apply when**: Every time. Non-negotiable.
**Minimum floor**: Give any nominee by a major auteur filmmaker (Lanthimos, Lynch, Anderson, Haneke, Noe, etc.) at least 5-8% minimum regardless of market price.

### Rule 2 -- Academy has extreme tail risk
**Date learned**: 2026-03-15
**Rule**: Never assign > 80% probability to any single Oscars winner, regardless of how dominant the guild consensus is.
**Why**: 2026 was a complete upset year. ALL frontrunners lost across multiple categories:
  - Best Picture: Bugonia won (not One Battle or Sinners)
  - Director: Chloe Zhao (not PTA, market had at 92.5%)
  - Actor: Timothee Chalamet (not MBJ, market had at 61.5%)
  - Supporting Actress: Elle Fanning (not Amy Madigan, at 55.5%)
  - Supporting Actor: Benicio Del Toro (not Sean Penn, at 77.5%)
**Apply when**: Any Oscars bet with confidence > 75%.

### Rule 3 -- Guild predictors weakened post-2020 Academy expansion
**Date learned**: 2026-03-15
**Rule**: SAG Ensemble -> Best Picture reliability dropped from ~65% to ~45-55%. DGA + PGA double -> ~50% (was 70%). The Academy's expanded international membership diverges from US guild consensus. Give auteur dark horses (Lanthimos, Lynch, Anderson) a minimum 5-8% floor.
**Why**: The Academy has dramatically expanded its membership since 2020, bringing in more international and arthouse voters who diverge from US guild consensus.
**Apply when**: Using SAG Ensemble as a predictor for Best Picture bets.
**Updated prior**: DGA + PGA double win -> ~50% reliable (was 70%). SAG alone -> ~45% reliable (was 60-65%).

### Rule 4 -- Size maximum 1-2% per category
**Date learned**: 2026-03-15
**Rule**: Never bet more than 2% bankroll on any entertainment category. The variance is too high relative to the edge.
**Why**: Even with a real edge (Sinners at 24% vs 20% market), one black-swan sweep wipes out many small wins.
**Apply when**: Any entertainment bet.

### Rule 5 -- Watch for "sweep" patterns
**Date learned**: 2026-03-15
**Rule**: When preliminary categories go to surprise winners at the start of a ceremony, increase tail risk estimates dramatically for remaining categories.
**Why**: Upset years tend to cluster. If Director and Actor go to surprises, Best Picture is also at elevated risk of upset.
**Apply when**: Live-betting during a ceremony.

## Lessons Learned

### Oscars 2026 -- Complete Sweep Upset
Bugonia won Best Picture, Chloe Zhao Director, Chalamet Actor, Fanning Supporting Actress, Del Toro Supporting Actor. All guild frontrunners lost. Key takeaway: the expanded Academy membership creates structural divergence from US guild consensus. Never trust guilds alone in the post-2020 era.

### Known Black Swan Events
- **2026**: Complete sweep upset -- all guild winners lost
- **2022**: CODA -- SAG winner won BP over The Power of the Dog despite BAFTA/DGA going to TPoD
- **2006**: Crash -- late surge won BP over Brokeback Mountain
